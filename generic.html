S&P 500 Stock Market Predictions

The S&P 500 Stock Market Predictions project was a Summer 2024 Data Science initiative involving Keshav Nayyar, Junaid Chaudhry, Elliott Eager, and Francis Fynnwilliams.

The project aimed to predict future stock prices using historical S&P 500 data and to analyze the performance of different sectors within the index. Using machine learning techniques, the team explored two main questions:
Can future stock prices be accurately predicted based on historical data?
How do sectors within the S&P 500 differ in performance?

Data was sourced from Kaggle and cleaned for analysis, focusing on companies currently in the S&P 500.

The project applied statistical tests like Chi-Squared, Z-tests, and ANOVA to assess sector volatility and performance. Results showed that some sectors, like Consumer Discretionary and Consumer Staples, outperformed, while others, like Finance and Industrial, were more stable.

Regression analysis was used for stock price predictions, with findings suggesting market volatility can affect prediction accuracy.

Overall, the project highlighted the potential of data science in financial forecasting and sector analysis.
